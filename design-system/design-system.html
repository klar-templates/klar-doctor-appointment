Rich text

The .prose wrapper styles CMS-rendered, long-form content — headings, paragraphs, links, lists, quotes, code, and images — so article bodies look right without per-element classes.

Designing for the long read

Great editorial layouts give words room to breathe. Generous line height, a comfortable measure, and a clear hierarchy keep readers moving from one idea to the next without friction.

What earns a reader's trust

Consistency is the quiet signal of craft. When every paragraph, pull quote, and caption follows the same rhythm, the writing feels considered — and considered writing keeps people on the page.

  • Set a measure of roughly 60–70 characters per line.
  • Anchor headings to the baseline with deliberate spacing.
  • Reserve emphasis for moments that genuinely matter.
Typography is what language looks like — and on the web, it is most of the design.

Inline notes like --leading-relaxed sit comfortably in a sentence, while images carry the same rounded corners as the rest of the system.

The Evolution of Fashion Influence

Traditional fashion journalism once held exclusive power over trend-setting and style validation. Magazine editors and critics were the sole arbiters of what counted as fashionable. The rise of digital platforms has fundamentally disrupted that hierarchy.

The anatomy of modern influence

Today's creators operate across multiple platforms, each serving a distinct purpose:

  • Instagram: visual storytelling through curated feeds and Stories
  • TikTok: trend creation and viral short-form content
  • Blogs: in-depth analysis and personal narratives
Influence in the digital age is not about gatekeeping access to style — it is about fostering communities and making fashion more accessible to everyone.

The democratization of fashion influence has permanently changed the industry, creating opportunities for diverse voices that were previously marginalized.
